About me

Hello! My name is Raúl Pedroche. Pleased to meet you. I am another student of RubyLearning.org's course "Programming for the Web with Ruby".

I like to be known by the handle "Pecio", but, truth be told, nobody ever calls me that.

I have a Tumblr web log that I do not update too much and a Twitter feed that I update somewhat more but is not too thrilling to read.

I am from Spain. I was raised in the northern coast, but now I am living in the capital, Madrid, in the center of the Iberian peninsula.

I am an unix administrator by training and trade, although I am currently between jobs. I am using this free time to try to learn more about programming. That is why I am taking Rubylearning.org course.

A confession: I am a Ruby newbie (although this is not my first contact with the language). But I think I am getting up to speed fairly well.

I learnt Object Oriented programming quite a long time ago (my main source was a book that used Objective-C back when nobody had heard of the language) and I have tried to use the paradigm everytime I have had to write some program. I have done OO programming in C++, Perl, and Objective-C (with Cocoa) and have used OO architectures in plain ANSI C.

Besides this course, I am doing a course on C# development. And it is like night and day. Quite often, while doing the exercises on C#, I cannot help but think how much easier and intuitive would it be in Ruby (or maybe any dynamic language).

(That and the fact I miss Vim, although Intellisense is great.)

It can be said I am under the influence of the Apple sect: my computers are Apple (besides a Sun Ultra 10 workstation I do not power on too often) and I have an iPhone. Although I do not have plans of buying an iPad (I have no use for it).

I suppose I have to talk about my hobbies. I am quite boring in that respect. I read books (mostly science fiction, but not exclusively), watch movies from time to time and listen to rock music.
